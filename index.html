When AI Is Unfair

BIAS: Systematic errors that lead to discriminatory outcomes

  • Real Examples:
  • Facial recognition: 34% higher error rates for dark-skinned faces
  • Hiring algorithms: Favoring male candidates for tech jobs
  • Image generators: Perpetuating stereotypes
Discussion: Where does bias come from?

When AI Makes Things Up

HALLUCINATIONS: AI generating false information with complete confidence

  • Real Examples:
  • ChatGPT citing fake research studies
  • AI creating incorrect math solutions
  • Made-up historical "facts" that sound real

The Fake Content Crisis

DISINFORMATION & DEEPFAKES: AI-generated content designed to deceive

  • Real Examples:
  • Deepfake videos of political figures
  • AI robocalls impersonating candidates
  • Fake viral images on social media
Impact: Threatens elections, spreads misinformation, harms individuals

Your Data at Risk

SECURITY & PRIVACY: Threats to personal information

  • Real Risks:
  • Student data entered into AI used for training
  • Data breaches exposing personal information
  • AI surveillance in schools
  • Companies selling conversation data

Laws Struggling to Keep Up

LEGAL CHALLENGES: Unresolved questions about ownership and rights

  • Key Issues:
  • Who owns AI-generated content? (Currently: no one)
  • Can AI be copyrighted? (Current answer: No human = no copyright)
  • Is using copyrighted work to train AI legal? (Courts deciding now)
  • Who's liable when AI causes harm?

Meet the AI Filtering Your Resume

ATS = Applicant Tracking System

  • The Reality:
  • Software that screens resumes BEFORE humans see them
  • Used by 98% of large organizations
  • 75% of resumes filtered out before human review
  • Why It Matters:
  • Your dream job could reject your application automatically
  • ATS looks for specific keywords, formatting, structure
  • A perfectly qualified candidate can be rejected due to ATS incompatibility
Example: If job posting requires "Project Management" and your resume says "Managed team projects," you might not match

Jobscan Demo: Matching Your Resume to a Job

  • STEP 1: Find a job posting you want to apply for
  • STEP 2: Go to jobscan.co
  • STEP 3: Upload your resume (PDF or paste text)
  • STEP 4: Paste the job description
  • STEP 5: Click "Scan Now"
  • STEP 6: Review your Match Score
  • STEP 7: Read the feedback in 5 areas: Searchability (keywords), Skills, Recruiter Tips (formatting), Formatting (ATS compatibility), Cover Letter analysis
  • STEP 8: Implement suggestions: Add missing keywords naturally, reorganize content if needed, fix formatting issues
  • STEP 9: Re-upload to verify improvements
Real Example: Job mentions "Project management," "stakeholder communication," "agile methodology." Your resume said: "Managed projects," "talked with clients," "flexible approach." Jobscan flags these mismatches. You revise to include actual job terminology. Match score goes from 45% to 78%
